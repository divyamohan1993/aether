Slide 2 · Problem

The tower fell. The dispatcher drowned.

First 24 hours of a Himalayan flood or landslide. Optical fibre snaps. Cell towers drop. Survivors fall back to crackly 2G voice notes. Standard Speech-to-Text hears static. One SDRF dispatcher takes a hundred calls an hour with a paper notepad.

254deadWayanad · July 2024 · one landslide night
868crackedJoshimath · January 2023 · buildings evacuated
12 hbatteryA radio chasing signal eats it in two
  1. i

    Cell tower is the first casualty.

    One bar of 2G EDGE, on and off. Streaming voice is impossible. Open connections die mid-syllable.

  2. ii

    STT shatters under stress.

    Wind, rain, sirens. A grandmother switching between Malayalam and broken Hindi. Standard pipelines return garbage.

  3. iii

    The phone is dying.

    Survivors reach hour ten on a battery that started at 50%. Local AI on the device finishes the job.

  4. iv

    Triage is a notebook.

    One human dispatcher. No structured fields. The most critical caller waits behind a wrong number.

Slide 3 · Value Proposition

A 40 KB cry for help. A two second answer.

A 40 KB SOS payload. Zero Speech-to-Text. Gemini 2.5 Flash hears the panic. SDRF receives a structured dispatch brief in under two seconds.

40KBFifteen seconds of voice · Opus 12 kbps · slips through one 2G burst
< 2secEnd-to-end · capture to dispatch JSON on SDRF screen
$0idleCloud Run scales to zero between events · pay per token only when invoked

Nothing streams. Nothing waits open. Nothing transcribes in the middle. The brittle four-stage pipeline (denoise, STT, translate, LLM) collapses to one resilient call.

Slide 4 · Underlying Magic

Four moves. One rescue brief.

Capture is local. Queue is offline. Burst is opportunistic. Triage is server-side. The phone never holds a Vertex AI key.

  1. 01 · Capture

    Press to record. Black screen.

    MediaRecorder · Opus · 12 kbps

    Pure black OLED UI. Pixels off. Volume key starts the recording so the screen never lights up.

  2. 02 · Queue

    Save first. Connect later.

    IndexedDB · ServiceWorker

    Clip lands in encrypted local storage before any radio wakes up. The payload outlives the connection.

  3. 03 · 2G Burst

    One handshake. One POST.

    HTTP · auto-retry · backoff

    OS reports a single bar. Aether fires the 40 KB POST, closes the socket, and returns to sleep.

  4. 04 · Triage

    Gemini hears the room.

    Vertex · 2.5 Flash · thinkingBudget 0

    Strict responseSchema returns a typed dispatch JSON. SDRF webhook fires within one second.

Slide 5 · Business Model

Public good first. Paid SLA second.

Aether ships humanitarian by default. Public agencies pay subsidised rates or nothing. Enterprises pay for guaranteed response time on the same pipeline.

Tier 1 · Public good

NDMA, SDMA, district authorities. Subsidised public-good deployment. Cost-recovery only. Pre-emptive deployment in high-risk districts.

Tier 2 · Enterprise SLA

Business continuity, field-ops, mining, pipelines. Paid SLA tier with response-time guarantee, custom webhooks, dedicated regions, signed audit trail.

Cost shape · Cloud Run scale-to-zero. Free-tier idle. Pay-per-token only when a survivor speaks. A district sitting quiet for a year costs zero rupees of compute.
Slide 6 · Go-to-Market

Three motions. One country.

India-first. Bharat-first. Distribution rides on existing public-safety chains, not on app-store discovery.

  1. Motion A

    NDMA Himalayan pilot

    One district in Uttarakhand or Himachal. Pre-monsoon. Field-tested with SDRF dispatchers. Outcome data feeds the next district.

  2. Motion B

    SDRF dispatcher console

    Web console at aether.dmj.one/tm. Hierarchical task tracker for the chain of command. Post-quantum auth from line one.

  3. Motion C

    NCC + civil defence

    Volunteer-network distribution. PWA, no install. Pre-loaded on training devices. Spreads through cadets, not ad spend.
